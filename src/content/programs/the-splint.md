---
title: "Splints I use"
description: "Learn about how physical orthotic splints are constructed and used to isolate movement during retraining."
order: 2
---

Splinting is a physical method used to temporarily restrict the movement of adjacent fingers. Because focal hand dystonia involves co-contraction and neural overlap, restricting non-affected fingers helps isolate the neural signals to the target finger.

The splints I construct and recommend are lightweight, custom-fitted, and non-painful. They serve as an educational guide for the motor cortex, showing the brain how to move one finger without causing others to collapse or curl.

> "By physically preventing co-contraction, a splint allows the brain to map pure, isolated movement signals once again."
