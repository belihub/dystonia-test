---
title: "Testimonial of a guitarist who overcame left (fretting) hand dystonia"
description: "A detailed recovery testimonial from a guitarist struggling with fretting hand coordination."
---

This case involved fretting-hand dystonia, where the ring and pinky fingers curled when placing the index and middle fingers on the guitar fretboard. This left-hand coordination loss is highly disruptive, particularly for complex chord transitions and speed playing.

Through slow tactile-input retraining, we retaught the brain to distinguish independent finger pressure. The client gradually rebuilt left-hand agility, resolving the involuntary flexion and returning to a full practice schedule.

> "The retraining process required patience, but isolating each finger's movement broke the pattern. My fretting hand is finally fluid and responsive again."
