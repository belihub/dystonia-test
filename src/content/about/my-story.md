As an active classical guitarist, I developed task-specific focal hand dystonia, which caused my right-hand index finger to clench involuntarily into my palm whenever I attempted to play. Like many, I was met with discouraging prognoses from medical specialists who declared there was no cure.

Refusing to accept a lifetime of not playing, I began researching motor learning, neuroplasticity, and biomechanics. Through trial, error, and meticulous observation of hand ergonomics, I designed a custom retraining protocol that successfully resolved my symptoms, allowing me to play and teach again.

> "By understanding that the brain has simply mismapped the movements, we can use targeted physical constraints and exercises to teach it the correct paths once more."
