My method focuses on retraining the brain's sensory-motor map. Dystonia is viewed not as a muscular defect, but as a "smudging" of the neural representation of the fingers in the motor cortex. By separating movement cues and introducing temporary physical guides (such as splints), we break the old dystonic patterns.

We work on slow, conscious, and pain-free movements. The retraining is highly customized, adapting to each musician's specific instrument, hand anatomy, and symptom presentation, combining kinesiology, ergonomics, and behavioral reinforcement.

> "Successful retraining relies on absolute consistency, sensory distinction, and never reinforcing the involuntary clenching during practice."
