---
title: "Some insights in F(H)D resolution (January-June 2016)"
description: "Key findings and practical observations gained from six months of intensive client retraining sessions."
---

During retraining sessions conducted in the first half of 2016, several crucial patterns emerged. First, the use of temporary orthotics (splints) to immobilize non-dystonic fingers proved highly effective in isolated retraining of the affected finger.

Second, success is highly dependent on mental fatigue management. Retraining sessions must be short, highly concentrated, and stopped immediately when focus declines, as tired practice reinforces the very dystonic paths we are trying to replace.

> "Shorter, highly-focused practice blocks prevent the brain from falling back into the default dystonic motor pattern."
